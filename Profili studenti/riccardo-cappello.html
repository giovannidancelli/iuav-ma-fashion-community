Corpus Terrae

Riccardo Cappello — Heritage — Living Archive

AUTHOR ↺

The project stems from the exploration of a profound connection between body, territory, and memory, developing from the ancient knowledge of Apulian transhumance. The collection interprets this legacy as a living practice, translating it into a contemporary vision in which time is stratified and transformed. The figure of the shepherd is reinterpreted as a modern wayfarer, balanced between the natural dimension and the urban context, bearer of an ever-evolving identity.

The garments take on an active function: not just aesthetic elements, but tools that accompany the body as it adapts, protects itself, and changes. Wool becomes an expressive language, while cowbells transform into audible signs of belonging, evoking a collective memory that persists in the present.

The project occupies a space between ritual and innovation, exploring the relationship between the archaic and the contemporary. Each piece represents a symbolic threshold, offering a reflection on embracing change without losing ties to one's roots.
